A monster is defined as any creature that can be interacted with and potentially fought and killed. Even something as harmless as a frog or as benevolent as a unicorn is a monster by this definition. The term also applies to humans, elves, dwarves, and other civilized folk who might be friends or rivals to the player characters. Most of the monsters that haunt the D&D world, however, are threats that are meant to be stopped: rampaging demons, conniving devils, soul-sucking undead, summoned elementals-the list goes on.
D&D Monster Manual 5ed. pp.4

ADVENTURERS STRIVE FOR MANY THINGS, including glory, knowledge, and justice. Many adventurers also seek something more tangible: fortune. Strands of golden chains, stacks of platinum coins, bejeweled crowns, enameled scepters, bolts of silk cloth, and powerful magic items all wait to be seized or unearthed by intrepid, treasure-seeking adventurers.Treasure comes in many forms, for example:

Coins: The most basic type of treasure is money, including copper pieces (cp), silver pieces (sp), electrum pieces (ep), gold pieces (gp), and platinum pieces (pp). Fifty coins of any type weigh 1 pound.

Gemstones: Gemstones are small, lightweight, and easily secured compared to their same value in coins.

Art Objects: Idols cast of solid gold, necklaces studded with precious stones, paintings of ancient kings, bejeweled dishes- art objects include all these and more.

Magic Items: Types of magic items include armor, potions , scrolls, rings, rods, staffs, wands, weapons, and wondrous items. Magic items also have rarities: common, uncommon, rare, very rare, and legendary.
Dungeon Master's Guide 5ed. pp.133

Welcome to the World of Dungeons and Dragons

Dungeons and Dragons is a tabletop pen and paper role playing game, where players take on the role of mighty adventurers who go on fantastical quests, fighting monsters, finding treasure, and saving the realm. One player, called the Dungeon Master (DM), instead controls the world and its inhabitants to provide interesting stories and frightening challenges for the players who adventure there. In particular, the DM has the responsibility of controlling the monsters that will do battle with the players.

Generating monsters that the players will fight has a number of steps and can take time to do manually, the purpose of our program is to expedite the process, and allow a DM to instantly generate a random monster for their players to fight based on a variety of parameters. In the following scenarios, you will take on the role of a dungeon master as he prepares encounters for the players in his game.
